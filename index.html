ABOUT ME

I am currently a research assistant at the National University of Singapore (NUS), affiliated with GLOW.AI and privileged to be supervised by Prof Bryan Kian Hsiang Low. Previously, I completed a MPhil in Economics from Oxford, where I was extremely fortunate to work with Prof David Hendry and Prof Jennifer Castle. Prior to Oxford, I earned a B.A. in mathematics-statistics from Columbia University and a B.Sc. (Hons) in computing mathematics from CityU Hong Kong.

My current research interests lie at the intersection of artificial intelligence (AI) and the social sciences, encompassing topics such as:

  • mechanism design;
  • LLM bias & mitigation;
  • AI for social science.

Beyond research, I am deeply enamoured with art and cultures, often drawing inspirations in the hidden corners of cities. This passion, dovetailed with my academic interests, culminates in a long-standing hobby of reflecting on societal dynamics, which occasionally inspires me to pen opinion pieces on pressing issues for local newspapers.
